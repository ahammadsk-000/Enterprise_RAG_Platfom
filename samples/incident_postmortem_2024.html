Incident Post-Mortem: INC-2024-07

Severity: High · Date: 14 July 2024 · Author: Omar Said · Reviewed by: Raj Patel

Summary

On 14 July 2024, the SentinelGuard analytics backend experienced a 42-minute partial outage in the US-East region. Approximately 18% of customers saw delayed alert delivery. No detections were lost because sensors buffered events locally, in line with the product's design.

Impact

Alert delivery latency rose from a normal median of 3 seconds to about 9 minutes during the incident. The customer portal remained available. The DataShield Cloud product was unaffected. The 99.9% uptime SLA was not breached for the month.

Root cause

A configuration change to the message queue reduced the consumer concurrency from 64 to 4, creating a processing backlog. The change passed code review but was not caught because the staging environment runs at lower traffic and did not reproduce the backlog.

Resolution

The on-call engineer, Dana Klein, identified the queue backlog within 11 minutes and reverted the configuration change. The backlog drained over the following 20 minutes and alert latency returned to normal by 15:02 UTC.

Action items

  • Add an alert on consumer concurrency dropping below 32. Owner: Engineering.
  • Run load tests against staging at production-equivalent traffic. Owner: Raj Patel.
  • Require a second reviewer for any change to queue configuration. Owner: People Ops.
  • Publish a customer-facing status note within 30 minutes of any High incident.

Lessons learned

Local sensor buffering worked exactly as designed and prevented data loss. The gap was in change validation: staging must mirror production load for infrastructure changes to be meaningful.
