SentinelGuard — Product FAQ

What is SentinelGuard?
SentinelGuard is Acme Corp's real-time network intrusion detection platform. It
inspects network traffic and uses behavioral analytics to flag anomalies such as
lateral movement, data exfiltration, and brute-force login attempts. It is designed
for security operations teams that need alerts in seconds rather than minutes.

How is SentinelGuard deployed?
SentinelGuard runs as a set of lightweight sensors deployed inside your network plus
a cloud-hosted analytics backend. Sensors are available as a Docker container, a
Kubernetes DaemonSet, or a hardware appliance. Most customers are fully deployed
within one business day.

What does SentinelGuard cost?
SentinelGuard is priced per protected seat on an annual contract. There are three
tiers: Standard, Professional, and Enterprise. The Enterprise tier adds single
sign-on, custom detection rules, and a dedicated customer success manager. Volume
discounts apply above 1,000 seats. Contact sales for a quote.

What is the uptime guarantee?
Enterprise customers receive a 99.9 percent uptime SLA for the analytics backend.
Critical incidents are acknowledged within 15 minutes, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.
Sensors continue buffering events locally if connectivity to the backend is lost, so
no detections are missed during a brief outage.

How are detections tuned?
SentinelGuard ships with hundreds of built-in detection rules mapped to the MITRE
ATT&CK framework. Professional and Enterprise customers can write custom rules in a
simple YAML format. Every rule can be set to alert-only or auto-block. False positives
can be suppressed per source, per rule, or per time window.

How does SentinelGuard integrate with other tools?
SentinelGuard exports alerts to Slack, PagerDuty, Splunk, and any generic webhook. It
also offers a REST API and a Python SDK so teams can build custom automations. The
DataShield Cloud product integrates natively so that encrypted stores are monitored
without exposing plaintext.

What support is included?
Standard includes email support during business hours. Professional adds a shared
Slack channel. Enterprise adds 24/7 phone support, the 99.9 percent SLA, and a named
customer success manager. All tiers have access to the documentation portal and the
community forum.

Who do I contact for security issues?
Report suspected vulnerabilities to the Acme security team through the customer portal.
Acme is SOC 2 Type II certified and GDPR compliant, and runs a private bug-bounty
program for Enterprise customers.
